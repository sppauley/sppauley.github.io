About Me

Sean Pauley is a Ph.D. student in the School of Journalism and Mass Communication and is pursuing a doctoral minor in African American Studies. Sean’s research focuses on both racial justice and methodology. His work primarily focuses on reparations for slavery, yet he has led several other projects on innovative methods across topics from survey methodology to Bayesian analysis.

Sean received his M.A. from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in Journalism and Mass Communication and his B.A. from Alma College in New Media Studies. While finishing his B.A. and M.A., Sean pursued public history work, such as serving as a solo developer for a web-based video game for the Grand Rapids Public Museum to showcase indigenous history around the Grand River. While at UW–Madison, Sean co-founded the GG Research Group in the School of Journalism and Mass Communication, which focuses on advancing video games research.

In his free time, Sean enjoys distance running, bouldering, pottery, and crocheting. He has programmed two video games with a friend and is currently working on a third.
